# Brand Voice — Solvea

## Core Identity

We are Solvea. We build the AI receptionist that never misses a call, never takes a sick day, and never puts a customer on hold.

We exist because every missed call is a missed customer. Every "sorry, we're closed" is revenue walking out the door. Every 3-minute hold time is a 1-star review waiting to happen.

We're not building "AI for the sake of AI." We're solving the most basic, most painful problem in service businesses: **the phone rings, and nobody's there to answer it.**

## Voice Attributes

### 1. Direct (not corporate)
- YES: "You're losing customers every time the phone rings and nobody picks up."
- NO: "Our innovative solution leverages cutting-edge AI to optimize customer engagement touchpoints."
- We say what we mean. No jargon. No buzzwords. Short sentences hit harder.

### 2. Empathetic (not preachy)
- YES: "We get it. You didn't start a medspa to spend your evenings returning voicemails."
- NO: "Businesses that fail to adopt AI will be left behind."
- We understand the pain because we've talked to hundreds of business owners living it.

### 3. Practical (not theoretical)
- YES: "Here's exactly how a plumber in Austin set up Solvea in 3 minutes and stopped missing after-hours calls."
- NO: "AI is transforming the future of customer communication paradigms."
- Every claim comes with a how, a number, or a real example.

### 4. Confident (not arrogant)
- YES: "Solvea handles phone, chat, SMS, and email from one AI agent. No other tool does this at $30/month."
- NO: "We are the world's best AI receptionist platform revolutionizing the industry."
- We let results speak. Customer data > superlatives.

### 5. Slightly Irreverent (not unprofessional)
- YES: "Your voicemail greeting isn't a customer experience strategy. It's a 'please call someone else' sign."
- NO: (pure dad jokes or memes)
- A little edge keeps us human. We can poke fun at outdated practices without being mean.

## Language Rules

- Default to active voice: "Solvea answers your calls" not "calls are answered by Solvea"
- Use "you" and "your" — talk TO the reader, not ABOUT them
- Numbers > adjectives: "97% first-contact response rate" not "incredibly high response rate"
- Use real industries: "medspa," "HVAC company," "law firm" — not "businesses" generically
- Contractions always: "you're," "don't," "it's" — we talk like humans
- No exclamation marks in excess. One per post max. Let the content carry the energy
- Avoid: "leverage," "utilize," "synergy," "revolutionize," "game-changer," "cutting-edge"
