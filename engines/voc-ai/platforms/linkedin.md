# LinkedIn — VOC AI Content Playbook

## Platform Rules

- **Character limit:** 3,000 per post (aim for 1,200-1,800)
- **Posting frequency:** 3-4x/week
- **Best posting times:** Tue-Thu 8-10am local time
- **Algorithm priority:** dwell time, comments > reactions

## Content Mix

| Type | Frequency | Purpose |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Data insight story | 1x/week | "We analyzed X and found..." — thought leadership |
| Founder / strategy lesson | 1x/week | Product research strategy, brand building with data |
| Industry trend | 1x/week | E-commerce shifts backed by review data |
| Contrarian / debate | 1x/2 weeks | Challenge conventional Amazon wisdom |

## Tone on LinkedIn

- Strategic thinker energy. Connect data to business decisions
- First person ("I") — personal stories outperform brand posts 10:1
- Paragraphs of 1-2 sentences. White space matters
- "I was wrong about..." and "Here's what the data actually shows" — signals intellectual honesty
- Story-data-story sandwich structure

## Hook Patterns

- "I spent 3 hours reading Amazon reviews last year. Then I analyzed 2 billion of them with AI. Here's what changed:"
- "We looked at the top 100 products in [category]. One pattern showed up in every single winner:"
- "Everyone in e-commerce talks about keywords. Almost nobody talks about sentiment. Here's why that's a mistake:"
- "A brand reached out to us after their 4.5-star product started losing market share. The answer was in their 3-star reviews:"

## Example Post

```
Last month a DTC brand asked us to figure out why their best-selling product was losing market share.

4.5 stars. 12,000+ reviews. Seemed healthy on the surface.

But when we ran sentiment analysis across their last 2,000 reviews, one pattern jumped out:

"Love the product but the new packaging is terrible."

This phrase — or variations of it — appeared in 28% of recent 3-star reviews. Up from 3% six months ago.

They'd changed their packaging to cut costs. Saved $0.40 per unit.

The review sentiment shift started 3 weeks after the change.

By the time it showed up in their star rating (4.5 → 4.3), they'd already lost 15% of the Buy Box rotation.

Here's what I learned:

→ Star ratings are lagging indicators. Sentiment shifts happen 4-6 weeks before ratings drop
→ 3-star reviews are your early warning system. They're specific enough to diagnose but not angry enough to exaggerate
→ $0.40 in savings cost them ~$200K in quarterly revenue

The data was there the whole time. In plain text. In reviews anyone could read.

They just weren't reading them the right way.

What's the most surprising thing you've ever found buried in customer reviews?
```

## What NOT to Do

- No "I'm excited to share" / "Thrilled to announce"
- No tagging 15 people for engagement bait
- No posting links in main post (put in first comment)
- No pure product announcements. Wrap everything in a story or insight
- No emoji spam
