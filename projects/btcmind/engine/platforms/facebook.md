# Facebook — VOC AI Content Playbook

## Platform Rules

- **Format:** Longer text posts, discussion-style, community groups + business page
- **Posting frequency:** 3-4x/week
- **Best times:** 9am, 1pm EST weekdays; 10am weekends
- **Algorithm:** Comments and shares. Real conversations get pushed to entire groups

## Two Strategies: Groups + Page

### Strategy 1: Facebook Groups (primary — highest ROI)

**Target groups:**
- Amazon FBA seller groups ("Amazon FBA Sellers," "Amazon Private Label," etc.)
- E-commerce communities ("Ecommerce Entrepreneurs," "Shopify & Amazon Sellers")
- Product research groups ("Product Research for Amazon FBA")
- DTC/brand builder groups

**The 10:1 Rule:** Post 10 genuinely helpful answers before mentioning your product. Be known as "the person who always has interesting data about reviews."

**How to contribute without pitching:**
- Answer product research questions with specific review insights
- Share anonymized data findings ("we looked at 50K reviews in [category] and found...")
- Help sellers interpret their own review data
- Only mention VOC AI if someone directly asks "what tool do you use?"

### Strategy 2: VOC AI Facebook Page (secondary)

- Share data visualizations and insights
- Post YouTube videos and blog content
- Customer success stories
- Free tool announcements

## Content Mix

| Type | Frequency | Purpose |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Community question | 1x/week | "For those doing product research — how do you use reviews?" |
| Data insight (anonymized) | 1x/week | Share findings from review analysis |
| Practical tip | 1x/week | "3 things I look for in competitor reviews" |
| Poll / survey | 1x/2 weeks | "What's your biggest challenge with product research?" |

## Tone

- Experienced seller helping fellow sellers
- First person — talk as a peer, not a vendor
- Warm, genuine, specific
- Match the group's energy

## Hook Patterns

- "Question for sellers — how much time do you spend reading reviews before launching?"
- "Found something interesting while analyzing [category] reviews this week..."
- "For anyone doing product research right now — here's a trick that saved me 10 hours"
- "Genuine poll: what's your #1 source for product research? Keywords, reviews, trends, or gut feeling?"

## What NOT to Do

- NEVER drop a product link as your first post in a group
- No corporate-speak
- No posting the same thing across 20 groups
- No fake questions designed to lead to your tool
- Don't argue in comments

## Example Posts

**Community Question:**
```
Question for FBA sellers here —

When you're doing product research, how much time do you actually 
spend reading competitor reviews?

I used to spend maybe 30 minutes skimming the top reviews. Then 
I started systematically analyzing them and realized I was missing 
90% of the useful information.

Specifically, 3-star reviews. Nobody reads them, but they contain 
the most specific feedback about what customers actually want 
improved.

What's your review research process like? Curious how others 
approach this.
```

**Data Insight:**
```
Interesting finding from this week —

We analyzed reviews across the top 100 products in the kitchen 
gadget space. One pattern that jumped out:

Products that addressed the #1 complaint from the category leader 
grew 3x faster in their first 6 months.

Not better marketing. Not lower prices. They literally fixed the 
thing customers were complaining about most.

Example: the bestselling garlic press had "handle breaks easily" 
in 23% of its 1-stars. The fastest-growing new entrant? Built 
specifically around a reinforced handle.

Sometimes the product brief is just... sitting there in the reviews.

Has anyone else found product ideas directly from competitor reviews?
```
