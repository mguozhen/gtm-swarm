# Threads — VOC AI Content Playbook

## Platform Rules

- **Format:** Text-first, 500 chars max per post, chain up to 10
- **Posting frequency:** 3-5x/week
- **Best times:** 9-11pm EST, 7-8am
- **Algorithm:** Replies and conversation depth. Shares to IG Stories boost reach

## Content Mix

| Type | Frequency | Purpose |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Data hot take | 2x/week | Surprising insight that sparks replies |
| Quick observation | 1x/week | "Noticed something in the data..." |
| Repurposed X banger | 1x/week | Top X content, even more casual |
| Question | 1x/week | "Genuine question for Amazon sellers..." |

## Tone

- Late-night analyst energy. Sharp observations, casual delivery
- Lowercase always. Even more relaxed than X
- "kinda wild that..." / "nobody talks about..." / "unpopular opinion:"
- NO selling. NO links. NO CTAs. Just be interesting

## Hook Patterns

- "kinda wild that most amazon sellers never read their competitor's 3-star reviews"
- "unpopular opinion: star ratings are the most useless metric in e-commerce"
- "nobody talks about this but the best product ideas come from angry customers"
- "hear me out... your competitor's worst reviews are your best product brief"
- "the difference between sellers making $50K and $500K? how they read reviews"

## What NOT to Do

- No links (Threads buries posts with links)
- No hashtags
- No product pitches. Ever
- No copy-pasting LinkedIn posts
- No engagement bait

## Example Posts

**Hot take:**
```
kinda wild that most amazon sellers spend 40 hours on keyword research

and zero hours analyzing what customers are actually complaining about

keywords tell you what people search for
reviews tell you what people actually want

different data. different outcomes
```

**Observation:**
```
noticed something in the review data this week

the products gaining market share the fastest aren't the cheapest ones

they're the ones that fixed the #1 complaint of the current bestseller

customers literally tell you how to beat the competition. for free. in their reviews
```

**Chain:**
```
Post 1:
unpopular opinion: the amazon product research industry is broken

Post 2:
everyone's chasing the same keyword data from the same tools finding the same "opportunities." then they're surprised when 47 sellers launch the same product

Post 3:
the sellers winning right now aren't looking at keywords. they're reading reviews. finding the exact thing customers hate about existing products. and building the fix

that data is free. and almost nobody uses it properly
```
