# Platform Tone — How Solvea's Voice Adapts

Same person, different room. Our [[brand-voice]] DNA stays constant — direct, empathetic, practical. But the energy shifts per platform.

## Tone Matrix

| Platform | Energy | Formality | Sentence Length | Emoji Use | "I" vs "We" |
|----------|--------|-----------|-----------------|-----------|--------------|
| X | punchy, fast | very low | short (5-15 words) | zero | "i" lowercase |
| LinkedIn | warm, thoughtful | medium | medium (10-25 words) | zero | "I" |
| YouTube | tutorial host | medium-low | conversational | zero | "I" |
| Instagram | bold, visual | low | short captions | minimal (1-2) | "We" |
| TikTok | raw, energetic | very low | spoken word pace | zero | "I" |
| Threads | casual opinion | very low | short | zero | "i" |
| Facebook | neighborly | medium | medium | zero | "We" |
| Newsletter | insider, detailed | medium | longer (15-30 words) | zero | "I" |

## Platform Personality Descriptions

**X:** The business owner at a bar after a conference. Speaks in sharp observations. Drops data casually. Says things other brands are afraid to say. Lowercase always.

**LinkedIn:** The founder giving a talk at a local business meetup. Shares personal stories. Connects experience to insight. Asks the audience real questions. Professional but not corporate.

**YouTube:** The friend who's really good with tech showing you something cool on their laptop. Patient but efficient. "Let me show you this" energy. Cuts the fluff.

**Instagram:** The designer who distills complex ideas into clean visuals. Bold claims. Numbered steps. Everything is scannable. Save-worthy.

**TikTok:** The founder filming a quick screen recording between meetings. Raw. Unpolished on purpose. "You have to see this" energy. Fast cuts.

**Threads:** The late-night thought from someone who can't stop thinking about their industry. Stream of consciousness but sharp. Hot takes welcome.

**Facebook:** The helpful member of a local business owners' group. Asks questions. Shares what worked. Invites discussion. Not selling — contributing.

**Newsletter:** The trusted advisor writing a weekly memo to their inner circle. Goes deeper than social. Shares things they wouldn't post publicly. Exclusive data and behind-the-scenes.

## Adaptation Examples

**Same insight, different tone:**

Core insight: "41% of calls come after business hours"

- **X:** "41% of your calls come after you've closed. your voicemail isn't a customer service strategy. it's a 'please call someone else' sign"
- **LinkedIn:** "We analyzed call patterns across 200+ service businesses. The finding that surprised me most: 41% of inbound calls arrive after 5pm. The after-hours caller is often your highest-intent buyer."
- **YouTube:** "Here's something most business owners don't realize — nearly half of your customer calls come after hours. Let me show you the data and what you can do about it."
- **Instagram:** Slide 1: "41% of Your Calls Come After Hours" / Slide 2: "Who's Answering Them?"
- **TikTok:** [screen recording of call analytics] "Look at this. See this spike? That's 6:30pm. After hours. 41% of all calls. And most businesses? They're sending these to voicemail."
- **Threads:** "kinda wild that most businesses are unreachable for the exact hours their customers actually want to call"
- **Facebook:** "Quick question for business owners here — have you ever checked what percentage of your calls come after you've closed? We found it's about 41% on average. How do you all handle after-hours calls?"
- **Newsletter:** "This week I want to share a data point that changed how I think about customer communication. When we looked at call patterns across our user base, 41% of inbound calls arrived after traditional business hours. Here's what that means and what the top-performing businesses are doing about it..."
