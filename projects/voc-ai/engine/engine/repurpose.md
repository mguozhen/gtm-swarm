# Repurpose Engine — 1 Topic → 10 Platform-Native Posts

One data insight goes in, platform-native posts come out. Each one RETHINKS the insight for that platform — not reformatted, completely rethought.

## The Repurposing Chain

```
DATA INSIGHT / TOPIC
    │
    ├─→ X: data thread OR contrarian singles (3-5 tweets)
    │       Angle: sharp data drops, "we analyzed X and found Y"
    │
    ├─→ LinkedIn: strategic narrative + business implications (1,200-1,500 words)
    │       Angle: "here's what this means for e-commerce," founder insight
    │
    ├─→ YouTube: research walkthrough or data deep-dive (8-12 min)
    │       Angle: live screen recording, "let me show you step by step"
    │
    ├─→ Instagram: 7-10 slide data carousel
    │       Angle: visual-first, one insight per slide, save-worthy
    │
    ├─→ TikTok: 30-60s screen recording or stat bomb
    │       Angle: "watch me find this in 30 seconds," raw demo
    │
    ├─→ Threads: casual data observation (1-3 posts)
    │       Angle: "kinda wild that..." late-night insight
    │
    ├─→ Facebook: community discussion post
    │       Angle: "has anyone else noticed..." invite seller input
    │
    └─→ Newsletter: weekly intelligence briefing (1,500 words)
            Angle: methodology + raw data + exclusive depth
```

## Rethinking vs Reformatting

**Example topic: "3-star reviews contain 34% of all product improvement ideas"**

- **X** gets the CONTRARIAN angle: "stop reading 1-star reviews. the real product briefs are in the 3-stars"
- **LinkedIn** gets the STRATEGY angle: "We analyzed review patterns across 500 categories. The finding that changed our product research methodology..."
- **YouTube** gets the TUTORIAL angle: "How to Find Product Opportunities in 3-Star Reviews (Live Demo)"
- **Instagram** gets the VISUAL angle: Slide 1: "Stop Reading 5-Star Reviews" → data breakdown per slide
- **TikTok** gets the DEMO angle: screen recording filtering 3-star reviews, pointing at a specific product brief
- **Threads** gets the OBSERVATION angle: "kinda wild that the most valuable reviews are the ones nobody reads"
- **Facebook** gets the COMMUNITY angle: "Question for sellers — how much time do you spend on 3-star reviews vs 1-star?"
- **Newsletter** gets the METHODOLOGY angle: full data breakdown, the segmentation process, raw numbers by category

## Production Workflow

### Step 1: Pick the Topic
Source topics from:
- New data analysis we've run
- Customer questions / support tickets
- Competitor news or product launches
- Trending Amazon categories or seasonal shifts
- Performing hooks from last week

### Step 2: Define the Core Insight
Every topic needs ONE core insight all platforms riff on.

Example topics for VOC AI:
- "3-star reviews are the most undervalued data source in product research"
- "The #1 complaint in [category] and the product opportunity it reveals"
- "Why keyword data alone leads to commodity products"
- "How the fastest-growing brands use review sentiment to differentiate"
- "The after-launch mistake: not monitoring review sentiment in real-time"

### Step 3: Run Through the Chain
```
Topic: [your topic]
Core insight: [your one-liner]

Produce platform-native posts for: X, LinkedIn, YouTube.
Each must rethink the topic. Not reformatted — different angle, structure, hook.
Reference [[hooks]] for opening lines.
Reference each platform file for rules.
Reference [[brand-voice]] for tone.
```

### Step 4: Review and Polish
- Does each post sound native to its platform?
- Are data points accurate? ([[brand-voice]] proof points)
- Are hooks strong enough?
- Does every post include at least one specific number?

### Step 5: Schedule
Follow [[scheduling]] for optimal timing. Stagger across the week.

## Content Multiplication Rules

- ONE data insight per week gets the full repurpose treatment
- Additional 2-3 "quick posts" per platform (reactive, trending)
- Every new analysis we run = automatic repurpose candidate
- Every customer "aha moment" = content topic
- Competitor launches = immediate reactive content opportunity
