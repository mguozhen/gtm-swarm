# X (Twitter) — VOC AI Content Playbook

## Platform Rules

- **Character limit:** 280 per tweet, threads up to 25 tweets
- **Posting frequency:** 5-7x/week minimum (mix of singles + threads)
- **Best posting times:** 8am, 12pm, 5pm EST (weekdays); 10am weekends
- **Algorithm priority:** replies, bookmarks, and retweets > likes

## Content Mix

| Type | Frequency | Purpose |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Data drop / insight | 3x/week | "We analyzed X reviews and found..." — stop the scroll |
| Seller tip / hack | 2x/week | Practical advice backed by data |
| Hot take | 1x/week | Contrarian opinion about Amazon selling / product research |
| Thread (5-12 tweets) | 1x/week | Deep data reveal or complete product research walkthrough |
| Tool demo (video/gif) | 1x/week | Show VOC AI finding an insight in real-time |

## Tone on X

- Lowercase casual. no caps unless emphasizing ONE word
- Short sentences. data hits hard
- Talk like a 7-figure seller texting a friend, not a SaaS brand posting content
- Numbers stop the scroll: "47,000 reviews. 1 insight. $340K in year-one revenue" hits differently
- Line breaks between every thought
- Let the data be the personality

## Thread Structure

Tweet 1 (Hook): Shocking data point or bold claim. Must work standalone
Tweet 2-3: What we looked at and why it matters
Tweet 4-8: The findings — specific numbers, examples, patterns
Tweet 9-10: What this means for sellers (actionable takeaway)
Final tweet: CTA — "follow for more data" or "try free: voc.ai"

## Hook Patterns That Work on X

- "we analyzed [X] reviews across [Y] products. here's what nobody's talking about:"
- "your competitor's 1-star reviews are a goldmine. here's why:"
- "i found your next product idea. it took 30 seconds"
- "the difference between a $50K product and a $500K product? they read the reviews differently"
- "stop guessing what customers want. 2 billion reviews already told you"
- "helium 10 shows you keywords. we show you what customers actually think. different game"

## What NOT to Do

- No hashtags
- No "excited to announce" corporate speak
- No threads that are just a blog post chopped into tweets
- Don't start with "I" — start with a data point or "you"
- No links in tweet 1 of a thread. Put link in last tweet or reply
- No vague claims without numbers

## Engagement Rules

- Reply to every comment in first 2 hours
- Quote tweet Amazon news with a data angle
- Jump into conversations about product research, Amazon selling, review management
- When someone asks "how do I find products to sell?" — reply with genuine value

## Example Posts

**Data drop:**
```
we analyzed 47,000 reviews across 200 garlic presses

the #1 complaint (in 23% of 1-star reviews): "handle broke after 3 months"

the #1 praise (in 67% of 5-star reviews): "easy to clean"

your winning garlic press: unbreakable handle + easy to clean

that's it. customers literally told you what to build
```

**Hot take:**
```
hot take: star ratings are useless for product research

a 4.3-star product and a 4.4-star product tell you nothing

but the SENTIMENT inside those reviews? that tells you everything

"great but slippery" is worth more than "4.3 stars"

stop counting stars. start reading feelings
```

**Seller tip:**
```
free product research hack that takes 60 seconds:

1. find your competitor's ASIN
2. run it through sentiment analysis
3. filter for 3-star reviews only

34% of 3-star reviews contain specific feature requests

that's customers writing your product brief for free
```
